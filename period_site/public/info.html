What is a period?

A menstrual cycle begins when you get your period or menstruate. Menstruation refers to the monthly shedding of the lining of your uterus. Menstrual blood flows from your uterus through your cervix and out of your body through your vagina. It’s a completely normal process that shows your body is working as it should! Menstrual cycles often last between 21 and 35 days.

Menstrual Phase (Days 1–5)

What Happens?

The uterine lining sheds through the vagina — this is what your period is. The blood you see is the lining that built up last month.

Why?

When the egg isn’t fertilized from the last cycle, estrogen levels drop, signaling the uterus to shed its lining.

Common Symptoms

  • Cramps
  • Fatigue
  • Lower back pain
  • Headaches
  • Mood changes

Self-Care Tips

  • Rest and gentle movement
  • Use heat therapy for cramps
  • Eat iron-rich foods
  • Stay hydrated

Follicular Phase (Days 6–14)

What Happens?

Your body prepares for ovulation. The pituitary gland releases FSH, which helps follicles in your ovaries mature. Usually, one follicle becomes dominant and gets ready to release an egg.

Why?

As the follicles develop, estrogen levels rise, thickening the uterine lining to create a nutrient-rich environment for a possible pregnancy.

Common Symptoms

  • Increased energy
  • Better mood and motivation
  • Clearer skin

Self-Care Tips

  • High-intensity workouts
  • Try new activities
  • Eat protein-rich foods
  • Be social and active

Ovulation (Around Day 14)

What Happens?

A mature egg is released from the ovary and travels through the fallopian tube — this is your most fertile time.

Why?

High estrogen triggers a surge of luteinizing hormone (LH), causing the dominant follicle to release its egg — this is ovulation.

Common Symptoms

  • Peak energy and confidence
  • Mild cramping on one side
  • Increased libido

Self-Care Tips

  • Intense workouts
  • Social events
  • Whole grains and fiber
  • Stay hydrated

Luteal Phase (Days 15–28)

What Happens?

After ovulation, the follicle becomes the corpus luteum, which produces progesterone. This hormone maintains the thickened uterine lining to prepare for possible pregnancy.

Why?

If pregnancy doesn’t occur, the corpus luteum breaks down, estrogen and progesterone drop, and the uterine lining begins to shed — starting the next cycle.

Common Symptoms

  • Irritability and mood swings
  • Bloating and water retention
  • Breast tenderness
  • Food cravings
  • Fatigue
  • Acne

Self-Care Tips

  • Low-intensity workouts
  • Comfort foods in moderation
  • Magnesium-rich foods
  • Relaxation and mindfulness
  • Go to bed earlier
